# Free Roofing Safety Document Sources

**Prepared for:** Mark Sheppard — Watkins Roofing (commercial / low-slope)
**Purpose:** A curated list of free, reputable sources for safety documents (JSAs/JHAs, toolbox talks, and lift inspection checklists) to attach to your app's Daily Progress Reports.
**Date compiled:** July 16, 2026

All links below were checked and resolve. Where a source is authoritative but gated (e.g., requires a free account or "add to cart" for a $0 item), that's noted so there are no surprises.

---

## How to use this list

- **JSAs/JHAs** = the pre-task hazard analysis for a specific scope of work (e.g., torch-down on a low-slope roof). Attach one per job/phase.
- **Toolbox talks** = short weekly/daily crew safety meetings with a sign-in sheet. Attach the talk + sign-in to the daily report.
- **Lift checklists** = the daily pre-use inspection you must complete before running rented equipment (telehandler, boom lift). Attach the completed checklist for each machine, each day.

The **⭐ BEST "download and use"** picks are flagged in each section.

## 1. JSAs / JHAs (Job Safety Analysis / Job Hazard Analysis)

### Authoritative / government (free, no login)

- ⭐ **OSHA — Job Hazard Analysis (Publication 3071).** The federal guide on how to build a JHA, with a sample worksheet inside. Authoritative reference. PDF.
  https://www.osha.gov/sites/default/files/publications/OSHA3071.pdf
- ⭐ **OSHA — JHA Worksheet (fillable).** The blank JHA worksheet by itself — clean, editable. PDF.
  https://www.osha.gov/sites/default/files/Job_Hazard_Analysis_Worksheet.pdf
- ⭐ **OSHA — JHA Template (Word / .docx).** Editable Word version you can brand as "Watkins Roofing" and fill per job. **Best editable starting point.**
  https://www.osha.gov/sites/default/files/job-hazard-analysis-template_final.docx
- **OSHA — Resources on Job Hazard Analysis (guide).** Supporting how-to reference. PDF.
  https://www.osha.gov/sites/default/files/resource_jha_guide.pdf
- **OSHA — Protecting Roofing Workers (Publication 3755).** Roofing-specific hazards and controls — great source material to populate the "hazard/control" columns of a roofing JHA. PDF.
  https://www.osha.gov/sites/default/files/publications/OSHA3755.pdf

### Roofing-industry specific

- ⭐ **NRCA — Job Hazard Analysis Template.** Roofing-specific JHA template organized by hazard topic areas. Priced at **$0.00** but delivered through the NRCA store (add to cart / free account; electronic download). The most roofing-tailored template on this list.
  https://www.nrca.net/safety/guidelines-resources/job-hazard-analysis-template
- **NRCA — Health & Safety hub.** Landing page for NRCA's free/low-cost safety resources (guidelines, tools, Spanish resources). Web.
  https://www.nrca.net/safety
- **Professional Roofing (NRCA magazine) — "Download NRCA's free job hazard analysis template."** Article that walks through and links the NRCA JHA template. Web.
  https://www.professionalroofing.net/WebExclusives/Story/download-nrcas-free-job-hazard-analysis-template/983

### Insurance / carrier safety libraries (free, roofing examples)

- ⭐ **CompSource Mutual — JSA: Commercial Roofing / Membrane Installation (Word).** A ready-made, editable commercial low-slope roofing JSA example — directly relevant to Watkins' work. .docx download.
  https://www.compsourcemutual.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/05/Job-safety-analysis-JSA-Commercial-roofing-membrane-installation-1.docx
- **CompSource Mutual — JSA for Residential Roofing.** Companion roofing JSA example (steep-slope). Web/reference.
  https://www.compsourcemutual.com/resources/safety-library/job-safety-analysis-jsa-for-residential-roofing/

**Note on templates vs. examples:** OSHA's Word/PDF files are *blank templates* you fill in. The NRCA and CompSource files are *roofing-specific* (NRCA = template; CompSource = worked examples you edit). Best practice: start from the OSHA editable template or NRCA template, then borrow hazard/control language from OSHA 3755 and the CompSource membrane-roofing JSA.

## 2. Toolbox Talks / Tailgate Safety Meetings (roofing-relevant)

### Best free libraries (government / research, no login)

- ⭐ **CPWR — Toolbox Talks (The Center for Construction Research & Training).** Large free library of one-page talks (falls, ladders, PPE, heat, silica, housekeeping, etc.), English & Spanish, print-ready PDFs with a discussion guide and sign-in. **Top pick for weekly talks.**
  https://www.cpwr.com/research/research-to-practice-r2p/r2p-library/toolbox-talks/
- ⭐ **CPWR — Stop Construction Falls: Safety Meeting Guides (Toolbox Talks).** Fall-focused talks — the single most relevant hazard for roofing. Free PDFs, EN/ES.
  https://stopconstructionfalls.com/training/toolbox-talks/
- ⭐ **NIOSH / CDC — Construction Toolbox Talks.** Government talks co-developed with CPWR; print-ready 8.5"x11" PDFs. Includes fall-prevention talks (e.g., Guardrails, 2022-146).
  https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/construction/toolbox-talks/index.html
- **OSHA — Fall Prevention Campaign: Training Resources.** Free fall-prevention training talks and materials (roofing emphasis). Web + PDFs.
  https://www.osha.gov/stop-falls/training-resources
- **OSHA — Stand-Down Resources for Employers & Employees.** Talks, handouts, and toolbox-talk material for the annual falls Stand-Down. Web + PDFs.
  https://www.osha.gov/stop-falls-stand-down/resources
- **OSHA — Fall Prevention Training Guide (Publication 3666).** A lesson plan built as three toolbox talks, with a dedicated roofing section. PDF.
  https://www.osha.gov/sites/default/files/publications/OSHA3666.pdf

### Other reputable free collections

- **Builders Mutual — Toolbox Talks (full-year PDF).** Insurance-carrier collection including roofing/fall-protection talks. Single PDF booklet.
  https://www.buildersmutual.com/downloads/BuildersMutual_ToolboxTalks.pdf
- **Washington State L&I (via ABC) — A Year's Worth of Weekly Safety Meeting Subjects.** 52 short tailgate talks, several roofing-relevant. PDF booklet.
  https://www.abc.org/Portals/1/Documents/Safety/Safety%20Best%20Practices%20Portal/Washington%20State%20LNI%20Toolbox%20Talks.pdf
- **OSHA Training Services — Toolbox Talks index.** Curated set of free talks organized by topic. Web.
  https://oshatraining.com/more-osha-training-resources/toolbox-talks-for-osha-safety-and-health/

**Coverage tip:** Between CPWR + NIOSH + OSHA Stop-Falls you can cover every topic you listed — fall protection, ladders, heat illness, hot work/torch (see CPWR "hot work"/fire and OSHA torch-down guidance), housekeeping, and PPE — for free, with sign-in sheets.

## 3. Aerial Lift / Telehandler Pre-Use Inspection Checklists (rented equipment)

You'll typically have two machine types on a commercial roofing job: a **SkyTrak / telehandler (rough-terrain forklift)** governed by OSHA **1910.178** (powered industrial trucks), and a **boom lift / MEWP** governed by **ANSI/SAIA A92** (and OSHA 1926.453 for aerial lifts). Use a forklift-style checklist for the telehandler and a MEWP checklist for the boom lift.

### Telehandler / rough-terrain forklift (OSHA 1910.178)

- ⭐ **OSHA — Sample Daily Checklists for Powered Industrial Trucks.** The authoritative sample forklift daily inspection checklists (internal-combustion + electric). Covers the 1910.178(q)(7) daily-inspection requirement. Web + printable.
  https://www.osha.gov/training/library/powered-industrial-trucks/checklist
- **OSHA — Forklift eTool: Pre-Operation Inspection.** Explains exactly what a pre-shift forklift inspection must cover. Web.
  https://www.osha.gov/etools/powered-industrial-trucks/operating-forklift/pre-operation
- **NIOSH / CDC — Conducting a Daily Inspection of Powered Industrial Trucks.** Government one-pager consistent with 1910.178(q)(7). Web + PDF.
  https://www.cdc.gov/niosh/docs/wp-solutions/2022-100/default.html
- **University of Washington Facilities — Telehandler / Rough-Terrain Forklift Pre-Use Inspection form.** Clean, ready-to-print PDF specific to telehandlers. **Good "download and use" telehandler sheet.**
  https://facilities.uw.edu/partner-resources/files/media/forklift-rough-terrain-pre-use-inspection-form.pdf

### Boom lift / MEWP (ANSI A92)

- ⭐ **IPAF — MEWP Pre-Use Inspection Checklist.** From the industry access-equipment authority; aligned to ANSI/SAIA A92. Free, print-ready. **Top pick for the boom lift.**
  https://www.ipaf.org/en/resource-library/mewp-pre-use-inspection-checklist
- **University of Washington Facilities — Boom-Supported Elevating Work Platform Pre-Use Inspection form.** Simple, ready-to-print boom-lift PDF.
  https://facilities.uw.edu/partner-resources/files/media/aerial-boom-lift-pre-use-inspection-form.pdf
- **Carnegie Mellon EHS — MEWP Pre-Use Inspection Checklist.** Another clean PDF you can adopt as-is.
  https://www.cmu.edu/ehs/Workplace-Construction/documents/mewp-pre-use-inspection-checklist.pdf

### Manufacturer resources (match the machine you actually rent)

- **Genie — Daily Pre-Operation Inspection Guide.** Manufacturer guidance for Genie booms/scissors/telehandlers. Web + PDF.
  https://www.genielift.com/en/aerialpros/daily-pre-operation-inspections
- **Genie — Pre-Operation Inspections (PDF).** Printable manufacturer inspection reference.
  https://www.genielift.com/docs/default-source/default-document-library/emear/en-gb/210x297-preoperation-inspections_2020-en.pdf
- **JLG — Service & Inspections.** JLG's official inspection resources and operator manuals (find your specific model's daily walk-around sheet). Web.
  https://www.jlg.com/en/parts-services/service-inspections/inspections

**Rental-company tip:** United Rentals, Sunbelt, and Herc supply a machine-specific operator's manual with the unit — it contains the manufacturer's daily inspection sheet, which is the "correct" one to use for that exact model. Ask the rental desk to include it, and use the checklists above as your standardized backup.

### Free OSHA On-Site Consultation (worth knowing about)

- **OSHA — On-Site Consultation Program directory.** Free, confidential 21(d) consultation for small employers — no citations, no penalties. A safety pro will review your site and programs at no cost. Find your state's program.
  https://www.osha.gov/consultation/directory-text

## 4. Ready-to-Use: Daily Pre-Use Safety Checklist for Rented Lift Equipment

Use this immediately, even before you download branded ones. It combines the OSHA 1910.178 (powered industrial truck) daily-inspection items for the **telehandler** and the ANSI A92 (MEWP) pre-use items for the **boom lift**. Complete it **for each machine, each day, before first use.** Take it out of service and tag it if any item fails.

> **How to use:** Print or screenshot into your Daily Progress Report. Check ✔ = OK, ✗ = defect (describe + remove from service). Engine-off checks first (walk-around), then engine-on/function checks.

### Header (fill in daily)

- Date / Shift: __________  Project / Address: __________
- Equipment type: ☐ SkyTrak / Telehandler   ☐ Boom Lift (MEWP)   ☐ Other: ______
- Make / Model / Unit #: __________  Rental Co.: __________  Hour meter: ______
- Operator (print & sign): __________  Trained/authorized? ☐ Yes  Certification current? ☐ Yes

### A. Walk-around — engine OFF (both machines)

- ☐ Tires / tracks: inflation, cuts, wear, wheel lugs tight
- ☐ No fluid leaks under machine (oil, hydraulic, fuel, coolant)
- ☐ Fluid levels OK: engine oil, hydraulic, coolant, fuel; battery secure/charged
- ☐ Hoses, cylinders, fittings: no leaks, chafing, or damage
- ☐ Forks/carriage or platform/basket: no cracks, bends, or damaged welds
- ☐ Chains, cables, and boom wear pads: intact, adjusted, lubricated
- ☐ Data/capacity plate and load chart present and legible
- ☐ All decals, warnings, and control labels present and readable
- ☐ Guards, covers, and counterweight secure; no missing hardware
- ☐ Seatbelt / restraint / lanyard anchor points present and undamaged
- ☐ Fire extinguisher present and charged (if equipped/required)
- ☐ Overall structure: no cracks, corrosion, or visible damage

### B. Operator station & safety devices

- ☐ Seat, seatbelt (telehandler) / harness anchor & gate (boom) functional
- ☐ Horn works
- ☐ Backup alarm and warning lights/beacon work
- ☐ Gauges/indicators and hour meter functional
- ☐ Steps, grab rails, and platform gate/chain secure and clean
- ☐ Emergency stop button works
- ☐ **Boom lift:** emergency lowering / auxiliary power tested and working
- ☐ **Telehandler:** load/moment indicator or stability system functional (if equipped)

### C. Function test — engine ON (in a clear area)

- ☐ Starts normally; no unusual noise, smoke, or vibration
- ☐ Service brakes hold; parking brake holds on a grade
- ☐ Steering responds normally (incl. crab/4-wheel modes on telehandler)
- ☐ Lift, lower, extend, retract operate smoothly through full range
- ☐ Tilt / frame level / outriggers or stabilizers deploy and hold
- ☐ **Boom:** rotate, articulate, jib, and platform level all function; controls self-center/return to neutral
- ☐ Boom/basket controls at platform AND ground controls both work
- ☐ No hydraulic drift when holding a raised load/platform
- ☐ Lights/work lights operate

### D. Worksite & operational checks (before lifting)

- ☐ Ground/surface firm, level, and rated for the load; no drop-offs or trenches
- ☐ Overhead clearances checked — power lines (maintain required clearance), structures
- ☐ Load within rated capacity for the boom angle/extension (check load chart)
- ☐ Travel path clear of workers, obstructions, and debris
- ☐ Weather acceptable (wind within rated limit; no lightning/ice)
- ☐ Fall protection worn and tied off in boom platform per manufacturer

### Sign-off

- Result: ☐ Machine SAFE to operate   ☐ Defects found — REMOVED from service & tagged
- Defects / notes: ________________________________________________
- Operator signature: __________  Supervisor (if defects): __________

> *This combined checklist is a general aid drawn from OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178(q)(7) (powered industrial trucks) and ANSI/SAIA A92 (MEWP) pre-use requirements. Always follow the specific operator's manual for the exact rented machine — it is the controlling document.*

## Quick-start recommendation

If you only grab three things today:
1. **OSHA JHA Word template** (Section 1) — brand it and use it per job.
2. **CPWR Toolbox Talks** (Section 2) — pick one talk per week; they include sign-in sheets.
3. **The combined lift checklist above** (Section 4) — plus the machine's manual from the rental desk.
